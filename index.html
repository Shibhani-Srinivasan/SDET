Works That Actually Shaped Me

CuCurls — My First Brand

What it was: A clean, affordable curly haircare brand I started and sold in 2021–2022.

What went right

  • Built an organic Insta following of 8.5k from scratch.
  • Sold products through DMs with packaging and personalized notes.
  • Ran profitable monthly sales cycles for ~6 months.

What went wrong

  • Expanded the target audience too soon (babies, moms, etc.), lost the niche, and sales dipped.
  • Shut down and sold the Insta handle for ₹8,000.

What I learned

  • Don’t dilute what’s working. Niche > scale. Obsess over the customer and be ruthless with product focus.

Leather Biz — Offline, Online, & Everything In Between

Background: My dad runs a leather tanning unit that sells material to local shops and walk-in buyers. Post-lockdown, everything halted—inventory piled up, and the usual retail model collapsed.

What I actually did

  • Shifted focus to B2B: created sample decks, bulk pricing charts, and digital catalogs.
  • Listed on IndiaMART and onboarded vendors directly—cold calls, follow-ups, and back-and-forth quotes.
  • Handled initial vendor messages and inquiries, posing as a full-time staffer—not the founder’s daughter—because in a male-dominated space, it was just easier to be taken seriously that way.
  • Figured out GST filing, invoice generation, and other tax basics because I had to.
  • Helped move stagnant inventory without discounting—just by reaching new channels.

What came out of it

  • Closed a few B2B deals that cleared old stock.
  • Built a lightweight, repeatable process for future orders.
  • Learned how traditional businesses operate—and how they change only when forced to.

What I learned

  • Sometimes the real work is unglamorous. Selling offline stock in a conservative, male-led industry taught me more about negotiation, trust, and systems than any MBA would.

Mystique Gifts — A Front Row Seat to Thoughtful Chaos

What it was: Janani runs Mystique Gifts—a creative gifting brand that works fast and beautifully. I didn’t have a title, but I jumped in wherever needed.

What I worked on

  • Assisted with hamper planning—sourcing, assembling, prepping for festivals.
  • Explored platform integrations for smoother order ops.
  • Did quick case studies on brands like The Messy Corner and OyeHappy to identify gaps and ideas.
  • Helped write Instagram captions, make memes, and structure a business deck.
  • Lived inside their backend chaos—and loved it.

What I got out of it

  • Real creative business moves fast, and systems matter more than they seem.
  • The best ideas often happen between packaging tape and DMs.
  • I got comfortable blending the creative with the operational—making it fun and functional.

Why it matters

  • This wasn’t a job—it was permission to play, learn, and contribute. That sandbox made me sharper, more instinctive, and confident in creative-led commerce.
