Works That Actually Shaped Me

CuCurls — My First Brand

What it was: A clean, affordable curly haircare brand for women that I started in 2021 and sold in 2022.

What went right

  • Built an organic Insta following of 8.5k from scratch.
  • Sold products through DMs with packaging and personalized notes.
  • Ran profitable monthly sales cycles for ~7 months.

What went wrong

  • Expanded the target audience too soon (babies, men, etc.), lost the niche, and sales dipped.
  • Exited the brand in profit and sold the Instagram handle for ₹8,000.

What I learned

Don’t dilute what’s working. Niche > scale. Obsess over the customer and be ruthless with product focus.

B2B Leather Ops – My Ground-Up Contribution in observing & Improving a Legacy Setup

Background: Managed and revamped my dad's leather tanning unit that sells material to local shops and walk-in buyers. Post-lockdown, everything halted—inventory piled up, and the usual retail model collapsed.

What I actually did

  • Shifted focus to B2B only model: created sample decks, bulk pricing charts, and digital catalogs.
  • Listed on IndiaMART and onboarded vendors directly—cold calls, follow-ups, and back-and-forth quotes.
  • Handled initial vendor messages and inquiries, posing as a full-time staffer—not the founder’s daughter—a conscious choice to cut through bias in a male-dominated industry.
  • Figured out GST filing, invoice generation, and other tax basics because I had to.
  • Helped move stagnant inventory without discounting—just by reaching new channels.

What came out of it

  • Closed a few B2B deals that cleared old stock.
  • Built a lightweight, repeatable process for future orders.
  • Learned how traditional businesses operate—and how they change only when forced to.

What I learned

Sometimes the real work is unglamorous. Selling offline stock in a conservative, operations heavy industry taught me more about negotiation, trust, and systems than any MBA would(without a degree).

Mystique Gifts — A Front Row Seat to Creative Strategy & Brand Intelligence

What it was: A hands-on creative freelancing at Mystique Gifts from Mar'24 - Mar'25, where I supported brand storytelling through gifting, social content, and strategic hamper curation for B2B and personal clients. I didn’t have a title, but I jumped in wherever needed.

What I worked on

  • Conducted competitive analysis of gifting platforms and developed proposals for onboarding, festive, and lifestyle hampers across tiers.
  • Curated 100+ themed hamper concepts for B2B clients like PayPal, Freshworks, ICEIL, and SRM Tech.
  • Created curated hamper pitch decks based on pricing, occasions and hyper-specific requirements (Personal Favs: Arangetram, Hangover, Dad-to-be).
  • Translated Sendoso’s enterprise gifting infrastructure into a modular framework deck and developed a strategic deck to evaluate Mystique’s B2B positioning and integration possibilities.
  • Created original LinkedIn and Instagram content and fun meme visuals tailored to brand personality—boosting relatability and organic shares across B2B and D2C segments.

What I got out of it

  • Developed design sensibility through hands-on curation, visual choices, and experience in crafting cohesive brand expressions.
  • Honed cross-functional fluency—creative curation, competitor intelligence, and brand expression.
  • Learned founder-led decision-making, pitch crafting, and content-market fit nuances from close quarters.
  • Learned how to rapidly theme and customize products around diverse client cultures, timelines, and budgets.
  • Practiced soft pitch strategy—balancing novelty and feasibility in product storytelling.

Why it matters

This was my creative playground. I worked closely with Janani Porkodi and got a front-row seat to how small businesses scale identity, ops, and delight. It honed my instincts for user experience, budget-smarts, and relationship nuance—skills I now carry into larger strategy roles.
